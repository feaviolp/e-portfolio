Hi Astrid,

Your chart looks good. We both took a similar approach (we even both independently selected 80 characters for the statement width).

It's interesting that you sequenced from least agree to most, whereas I did the reverse. Either is fine and I think both make interpreting the data easier.

A change I would make to yours would be to put No Opinion in the midldle of each bar so you can visually see the strength of agreement (on the left) and the strength of disagreement (on the right). Mine was originally sequenced the same as yours but I found that I was able to force it to the sequence I wanted using factor().

I like the colours that you used. I stuck with the default colours which are perhaps a bit too bright for this kind of paper.

I'm interested to know what structure you used for your data file. I started out with:

Statement              Agree     No Opinion   Disagree

aaaa                        10               10              10

bbbb                        10               10              10  

But I found that I was unable to plot the chart correctly (no doubt my own fault). so I changed it to:

Statement           Answer     Count

aaaaa                 Agree           10

aaaaa                 Disagree      10

bbbbb                 Agree           10

With this format I was able to produce the chart, but I was unable to sequence the statements based upon the count of Agree. I could only get alphabetical or based upon ALL responses (total responses for each question). I ended up fudging it by adding an alphabetical prefix to get the sequence that I wanted but you seem to have cracked doing it properly. Well done!

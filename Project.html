MSc Computing Project

A project that explores the use of ontology to identify bias and increase fairness when using machine learning to predict recidivism

This module aims to:

  1. Enable students to plan and undertake an independent technical project into a specific topic of their choice.
  2. Further develop and enhance students’ technical skills such as coding, programming and algorithm development skills.
  3. Develop students’ transferable skills, including time and project management, organisational skills, data collection and analysis, and communication skills.
  4. Develop students’ critical thinking skills.
  5. Develop students’ understanding of their chosen topic to an advanced level, considering legal and ethical issues surrounding this topic.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Systematically identify research problem(s) within a suitable research topic involving computing.
  2. Carry out, structure, and present effectively a focused critical and analytical reading and synthesis of accessed information on the topic.
  3. Articulate the professional, legal, ethical and social issues related to the chosen topic and follow relevant professional codes of conduct.
  4. Evaluate critically the research conducted and present the resulting artefact as a form of a defence of the research.

Reflections

Unit 1: An Introduction to Your Dissertation


I was unsure how the project module was going to be delivered. So far, prior to the start of the module, I have:

  • Propsed a project
  • Discussed and refined the idea with Steph, the tutor from the Research Methods and Professional Practice module
  • Secured a first supervisor; Godfried Williams, because the proposal includes producing an ontology and Godfried taught the Knowledge Representation and Reasonoing module
  • Discussed the proposal with Godfried and further refined it
  • Secured a second supervisor; Sammy Danso, who taught the Introduction to Artificial Intelligence module and who was initially going to teach the Knowledge Representation and Reasonng module before being replaced by Godfried, hence I assume also knowledgeable about ontologies.
I was concerned that the project would be entirely self directed. However, there is still some structure for the first few weeks to produce and agree the project proposal, and there is a minimum number of supervisor meetings that I can call upon, so there is more structure than I was expecting, which is good.

The unit started with a lecturecast covering the elements of a dissertation and, at a very high level, how to approach it. There was also some reading that went over how to present a report.

By far the biggest benefit of this unit was looking ahead at the structure for the rest of the module so I can prepare!

Unit 4: Reviewing Literature


The lecurecast and reading had some useful pointers for the literature review. Bloom's taxonomy (Bloom, 1956) showed a logical evolution of a literature review as: descriptive -> interpretation -> analysis -> synthesis. It was also useful to get some guidance on the ratio of good sources, with the recommendation being at least 60% from peer reviewed sources and up to 40% from other sources. Another useful tip was to create a table of literature themes to structure the review, which I will try to do. Finally, there was a reminder of the need to be critical in the review and not just submit a collection of references.

Unit 6: Intellectual Property


This was a light week, with the lecturecast and reading covering intellectual property in the form of patents, copyrights and trademarks. Most I was already aware of and I don't believe that any will be particularly relevant to my dissertation.

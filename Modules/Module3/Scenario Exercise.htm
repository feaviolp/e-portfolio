Scenario Based Exercise

Here’s my attempt at the scenario based exercise this week for formative feedback:

 

First I created three vectors, one for each group of employees.

> group1 <- c(45,29,56,52,45,45,41)

> group2 <- c(61,53,41,58,53,47,44)

> group3 <- c(35,21,33,27,22,26,30)

 

Next find the mean of each group:

> mean(group1)

[1] 44.71429

> mean(group2)

[1] 51

> mean(group3)

[1] 27.71429

 

Now run a Shapiro test on each group to check for normal distribution:

> shapiro.test(group1)

 

            Shapiro-Wilk normality test

 

data:  group1

W = 0.92044, p-value = 0.4728

 

> shapiro.test(group2)

 

            Shapiro-Wilk normality test

 

data:  group2

W = 0.95666, p-value = 0.7896

 

> shapiro.test(group3)

 

            Shapiro-Wilk normality test

 

data:  group3

W = 0.95142, p-value = 0.7426

 

Since each group has a normal distribution (p-value greater than 0.05) we’ll run a one sample t-test on each group. I picked a number for mu (25) slighly lower than the lowest mean of the three groups (27).

H0: There is no increase in efficiency in the target group from the baseline of 25

Ha: There is an increase in efficiency in the target group from the baselie of 25.


> t.test(group1, mu=25, alternative='greater')

 

            One Sample t-test

 

data:  group1

t = 6.081, df = 6, p-value = 0.0004494

alternative hypothesis: true mean is greater than 25

95 percent confidence interval:

38.4146     Inf

sample estimates:

mean of x

 44.71429

 

> t.test(group2, mu=25, alternative='greater')

 

            One Sample t-test

 

data:  group2

t = 9.3901, df = 6, p-value = 4.141e-05

alternative hypothesis: true mean is greater than 25

95 percent confidence interval:

45.61958      Inf

sample estimates:

mean of x

       51

 

> t.test(group3, mu=25, alternative='greater')

 

            One Sample t-test

 

data:  group3

t = 1.3595, df = 6, p-value = 0.1114

alternative hypothesis: true mean is greater than 25

95 percent confidence interval:

23.83454      Inf

sample estimates:

mean of x

 27.71429

 

As expected, group 2 (which had the highest mean efficiency) had the most significant increase in efficiency with a p-value of 4.141e-05 and 95% confidence interval mean is greater than 45.61958.

Next was group 1 (with te hmiddle mean effiency score) with a p-value of 0.0004494 and 95% confidence interval mean is greater than 38.4146.

Finally group 3 was last with a p-value of 0.1114 (not significant) and 95% confidence interval mean is greater than 23.83454.

The 4th Industrial Revolution - Initial Post

In my initial post I described how Boeing made critical mistakes in their initial launch of the 737 MAX aircraft, leading to two crashes, 346 lives lost and a financial cost of $19bn (Rushe, 2020). The brief asked for a “significant impact” of an information systems failure and I don’t think it gets much more significant than 346 people being killed.

In order to bring a new plane to market faster Boeing adapted an existing plane and masked it’s different handling characteristics with a manoeuvring characteristics augmentation system (MCAS), which they failed to disclose (Travis 2019). This is a case of using technology “because you can” without considering the vital role of the human in the system.

Adeniyi (2023) and van Toor (2023) were both shocked by the events and agreed that lessons should be learned, which in fact they have, to a point, as van Toor (2023) confirms when noting that plane is now approved as safe after modifications have been made (Federal Aviation Administration, 2020).

Turning to Fintech, Adeniyi (2023) described the Capital One data breach where the information of 106 million customers was compromised (CBS, 2019). Sadly this is not an isolated incident, as a noted in my reply (Feaviour, 2023) where Wikipedia (N.D.) lists 370 separate data breaches since 2004, including many well-known brands.

Similarly, van Toor (2023) described the LastPass data breach, putting all 33 million LastPass users at risk (Kapko, 2023).

The two data breaches are examples of failings in organisations trusted with some of our most valuable data; financial and passwords.

As Industry 4.0 continues to advance, connect and automate, there is no doubt that we will be able to achieve things hitherto thought impossible. However, care must be taken because each new opportunity brings potential risks that must be managed!

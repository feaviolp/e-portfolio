Knowledge Representation is a recent phenomenon - Peer Responses

Hi Astrid,

I agree with you that OWL2 is the best choice to express ontologies for software agents on the WWW. I too noted that OWL-Lite is in fact already a subset of OWL2, and so the comparison is a little flawed (Feaviour, 2023). I therefore chose to compare OWL-Full, OWL-DL and OWL-Lite to try to decide on the best variant of OWL. I concluded that OWL-Lite wouldn't be suitable because it is too simple and not very expressive; it's main benefit is simplicity.

I felt that the choice between OWL-DL and OWL-Full was less clear cut though. Intuitively we might consider that the more expressive the tool is the better it would be at representing all of the intricacies of the WWW. However, Kalibatiene & Vasilecas (2011) note that the high expressiveness of OWL-Full means that is losses the ability to perform complete reasoning, which would count against an ontology expressing the WWW. So I think it is contextual between OWL-DL and OWL-Full, depending upon the level of detail required to be expressed, how much much reliance is placed on reasoning, and what you start with. 

If starting from scratch I would say OWL-DL could be a good choice because it allows complete reasoning. If starting with an existing RDF schema, OWL-Full is probably best because it has complete compatibility with RDF so the schema could be imported.

Agent Based Systems - Initial Post

Referring briefly to the reading; Weststeijn (2011), I found it somewhat interesting to see the efforts to try to deduce the origins of languages and which languages inspired which. However, the objective to trace back to the “primitive language” spoken by Adam was an exercise in futility driven by ulterior motives which I would have spoken more about here had the conclusion not already delivered a similar punchline. Suffice to say that I do not find this to be compelling evidence of ancient knowledge representation.

The term “knowledge representation” seems to be relatively new and created for the field of artificial intelligence (AI). I was unable to trace back any further than Newell and Simon (1972) who discuss structuring and encoding information as representing knowledge. However, the desire to represent knowledge can be traced back much further. Venn (1881) discussed symbolic logic as a way of representing logical relationships, including the now famous Venn diagram. He was building upon work by Boole (1854), who proposed that symbols could be used to represent logic, and gave us Boolean algebra. And Boole even acknowledged Aristotle, who lived 384-322 BC, turning his rules into equations. So it is clear that the desire to represent knowledge far predates computer science, but the term knowledge representation is a more recent invention aligned with AI.

Reasoning takes the represented knowledge and makes it actionable when the knowledge is incomplete. In other words, it is not necessary to specify every last detail as long as there is sufficient knowledge to reason an outcome through deduction, induction, analogy etc. (Xue & Zhu, 2009). Brachman & Levesque (2004) described this as a system using information to create a set of beliefs which go beyond the limits of the facts it was given. In the field of AI, it would therefore seem that reasoning is the critical additional part to elevate a system to one of “learning” or “intelligence”. Knowledge representation on its own is simply a method for collecting, structuring and encoding information.

That said, having encoded the information into a knowledge base, there is still value in simply being able to accurately and unambiguously interrogate the information without any additional reasoning. If that were not the case then the scholars mentioned above; Aristotle, Boole and Venn, would have had little interest in their work, and all are famous names today. So knowledge representation on it’s own does indeed have value, but reasoning elevates it to a higher level.

Research Methods and Professional Practice

A practical foundation in research methods to enable research in practical settings.

This module aims to provide the ability to:

  1. Study and reflect on key principles and methods in research based on the scientific method and relevant to various disciplines.
  2. Examine various research strategies and designs as applicable to projects at hand.
  3. Develop research competencies, in particular those relating to the collection and analysis of data types to enable a critical design and evaluation of independent research.
  4. Take a reflective and independent approach to the learning process.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Appraise the professional, legal, social, cultural and ethical issues that affect computing professionals.
  2. Appraise the principles of academic investigation, applying them to a research topic in the applicable computing field.
  3. Evaluate critically existing literature, research design and methodology for the chosen topic, including data analysis processes.
  4. Produce and evaluate critically the resulting research proposal for the chosen topic.

Reflections

Unit 1: Scientific Investigation and Ethics


My first thought when reviewing the content of this module was "wow, it's going to be a lot of work". On a personal note I was informed last week that I'm being made redundant from the company I have worked for for 34 years so I'm feeling anxious on many levels. I'm definitly going to need to get into a good headspace for this module.

The first seminar was useful because on one hand it confirmed that it's going to be an intensive module, but it also helped to position the module, and the fact that it runs over the Christmas break and so gives a little more time sounds helpful. The tutor, Steph Paladini, seems helpful too. One recommendation from the seminar was to start using a references manager. I've been doing references manually up to now, but looking at the quantity of references that will be needed for this module, and even more for the final project, I will certainly be looking at that and have downloaded Mendeley to give it a try.

The reading covered ethics in research, and also the nature of research such as the difference between inductive and deductive reasoning (which I still need to think carefully about to differentiate). There was also a short quiz, but the questions were practically the same as in one of the required readings, although it was still good to practice. Although there was a lot of reading, especially for the first week in a new module, it was actually relatively simple, so I'm feeling a bit less anxious about the module already (I still foresee a huge amount of work though).

It put this module into context very well. In fact I think it would've been good to have in unit 1, but unit 2 isn't far behind so not a real problem.

The reading, mainly from the core textbook, described why a literature review is important in projects and how to go about it, which is clearly going to be very useful.

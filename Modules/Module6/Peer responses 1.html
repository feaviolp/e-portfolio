Agent Based Systems - Peer Responses

Astrid van Toor

Hi Astrid,

You gave some good examples where agent-based systems are being used to good effect. What I found particularly interesting though was how you moved seamlessly between “active” agents and “modelling” agents (my terms). In other words agents performing real tasks such as traffic management and supply chain management, and agents modelling real-world scenarios such as epidemic spread predictions and climate modelling.  

In my own initial post (Feaviour, 2023) I discussed agents for managing supply chain activities as well as different agents to model them, Kanakavelu (2023) discussed agents in healthcare for both screening and modelling, and Rizzo (2023) discussed agents to navigate in space or underwater plus biological modelling agents. In practical terms the activity based use cases, existing as they do in our physical world, are very different from the modelling use cases existing entirely in an artificial world. But as agent-based systems there is hardly any difference, to the point that most people didn’t even differentiate during this collaborative discussion. It made me think anyway…

Samuel Adeniyi

Hi Sam,

I agree, in general, with your conclusion that the rise of agent-based systems can be attributed to the increasing complexity of modern business environments. I certainly agree that the increasing complexity has created new opportunities for the use of agent-based systems, but I would also suggest that even without the increasing complexities there would still be opportunities for agent-based systems to provide some of the benefits that you mentioned. I suspect that’s what you meant and mine is perhaps a pedantic point, but I thought it worth making.

Your comment about agent-based systems being suited to working with incomplete information is interesting. With ever more data being collected, including the explosion of IoT devices, it’s reasonable to assume that there will be more and more gaps in the ever increasing sea of data. It seems likely that agents will therefore continue to play an ever increasing role in systems architecture.
